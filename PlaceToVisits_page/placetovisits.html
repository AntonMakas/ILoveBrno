Discover the Best of Brno

Introduction

Brno, the heart of Moravia, offers a perfect blend of history, innovation, and vibrant culture. Whether you're exploring medieval landmarks or enjoying its trendy cafes, Brno has something for everyone. Let’s uncover its top attractions!

Top Highlights

  • Špilberk Castle

    Perched atop a hill overlooking Brno, Špilberk Castle is a landmark steeped in history and culture. Originally built in the 13th century as a royal fortress, it evolved into a baroque citadel and later served as a notorious prison known as the "Jail of Nations." Today, Špilberk Castle is a vibrant cultural hub, hosting the Brno City Museum, exhibitions, and various cultural events. Visitors can explore its historic dungeons, take in the panoramic views of Brno from its ramparts, or simply enjoy a peaceful stroll through the surrounding park.

    Address: Špilberk 210/1, 662 24 Brno
    Opening Hours: 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM
    Tickets: Adults 150 CZK, Students 100 CZK

  • Cathedral of St. Peter and Paul

    Dominating Brno’s skyline, the Cathedral of St. Peter and Paul is a stunning example of Gothic Revival architecture. With origins dating back to the 12th century, the cathedral has witnessed centuries of history and stands as a symbol of Brno. Its intricate interiors feature soaring arches, stained glass windows, and exquisite sculptures. For those who love breathtaking views, a climb to the top of its twin towers offers panoramic vistas of the city.

    Address: Petrov 9, 602 00 Brno
    Opening Hours:Monday – Saturday: 8:15 AM – 6:30 PM and Sunday: 7:00 AM – 6:30 PM
    Tickets: Free entry, tower access 70 CZK

  • Villa Tugendhat

    Designed by the legendary architect Ludwig Mies van der Rohe in 1929, Villa Tugendhat is a masterpiece of modernist architecture and a must-visit for art and design enthusiasts. Its minimalist design, open spaces, and innovative use of materials (such as onyx and exotic woods) were revolutionary for its time. As a UNESCO World Heritage Site, it offers a glimpse into the life of the Tugendhat family, as well as the architectural advancements of the early 20th century

    Address: Černopolní 45, 613 00 Brno
    Opening Hours: Tuesday – Sunday: 10:00 AM – 6:00 PM
    Tickets: Standard Tour 350 CZK, Extended Tour 500 CZK

Historical Landmarks

  • Old Town Hall (Stará Radnice)

    Discover the story behind the crooked spire and the legendary Brno Dragon, housed in the city’s oldest secular building. Image: A shot of the Old Town Hall with its crooked spire or the Brno Dragon sculpture.

    Address: Radnická 8
    Opening Hours: 9:00 AM – 6:00 PM
    Tickets: 70 CZK (Tower Access)

  • Capuchin Crypt

    An eerie yet fascinating crypt where mummified remains of Capuchin monks lie preserved, offering a glimpse into Brno’s spiritual history.

    Address: Kapucínské náměstí 5
    Opening Hours: 10:00 AM – 5:00 PM
    Tickets: 60 CZK
